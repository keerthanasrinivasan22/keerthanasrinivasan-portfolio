Keerthana – Where Curiosity Meets Code

I’m Keerthana Srinivasan — a data-driven thinker and creative problem solver, currently pursuing my Master’s in Data Science at the University of North Texas. I’m passionate about building intelligent systems that don’t just analyze the world — they interact with it. From guiding people through immersive AR/VR applications to training machine learning models that detect patterns in complex, high-dimensional data, I enjoy crafting solutions that live at the intersection of insight, usability, and innovation.

Alongside my graduate studies, I currently work as a Teaching Assistant for Software Engineering and AR/VR courses, where I mentor students in hands-on lab work, Unity development, and mixed reality projects using tools like MRTK3, Photon, and Vuforia. I also serve as a Machine Learning Research Assistant, contributing to projects such as chatbot systems for accessibility, mental wellness tools powered by EEG data (Emotiv), and generative AI solutions that enhance user experience and system intelligence.

Since then, I’ve built and deployed projects in areas like fraud detection, medical image analysis, satellite communication systems, and spatial computing — often leading them from end-to-end with a focus on both accuracy and real-world impact.

I love turning abstract data into actionable insights and blending emerging technologies with human-centered design. With hands-on experience in tools like Python, TensorFlow, SQL, Tableau, Unity, AWS, Hugging Face, and Streamlit, I thrive in fast-paced, cross-functional environments where innovation happens at the edge of curiosity and code.

If there’s one thing I’ve learned, it’s that great systems aren’t just built — they’re thoughtfully engineered, line by line, with empathy and intention at the core.
